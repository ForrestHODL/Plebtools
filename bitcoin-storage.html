Where should you keep your Bitcoin?

Become your own bank with a definitive guide to safe storage of your bitcoin.

Exchanges

Exchanges are a place where you can purchase bitcoin. When you purchase bitcoin on an exchange, they will hold the bitcoin for you. This is not advised due to risks with insolvency, loss of access, and paper bitcoin. When buying from an exchange, you should always withdraw to your own wallet.

Centralized Exchanges

Platforms like Coinbase, Shakepay and Kraken. Easy to use, but you don't really own your bitcoin until you withdraw to your own wallet.

Decentralized Exchanges

P2P (Peer-to-peer) platforms where you trade directly with others, like RoboSats. You control your funds, but may require more technical knowledge.

Non-custodial exchanges

Non-custodial exchanges take payment and send bitcoin directly to your wallet address. Bitcoin Well is an example of a non-custodial exchange.

Hot vs Cold Storage

Now that you've decided to take your bitcoin off of the exchange, what wallet should you pick? People talk about cold storage vs hot wallets, what are the actual differences?

Hot Wallets


Hot Wallets are bitcoin wallets where the keys are kept on a device connected to the internet. For example, if you create a new wallet with Sparrow, the keys will be stored on the device you create it on. Hot wallets are useful for everyday spending, like carrying cash in a physical wallet.

Cold Wallets


Cold wallets are wallets where the keys are kept offline. You can use a designated hardware wallet for this, or even an air gapped computer with no internet connection. Cold wallets are like a vault for your savings.

Hardware Wallets

Hardware wallets are dedicated devices that keep your private keys offline for strong protection against hacks and malware.
A key consideration when choosing a hardware wallet is making sure the firmware is bitcoin-only. This can reduce the points of attack if someone attempts to steal your keys.

Popular brands include the Blockstream Jade (affiliate link below), the Trezor Safe 3/5 (bitcoin-only edition), and the Coldcard Q/Mk4.
